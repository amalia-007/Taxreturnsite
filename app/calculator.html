How to Lodge Your Tax Return

7 steps to lodge via ATO myTax — takes about 20 minutes.

2024-25

Go to my.gov.au and sign in with your username and password, or create a free account if you don't have one. You'll need a valid email address and a mobile number for SMS verification. Once logged in, you'll see your myGov inbox and linked services. Make sure you use the same account you used previously — creating a duplicate account will cause problems. If you've forgotten your password, use the "Forgot password" link on the login page.

From the myGov home screen, click Services, then Link a service, and select Australian Taxation Office. You'll be asked to verify your identity using your Tax File Number (TFN), date of birth, and a document such as your Notice of Assessment. If you've never lodged before, use your TFN and bank account details. The linking process takes 2–3 minutes. Once linked, "Australian Taxation Office" will appear on your myGov home screen as a tile.

→ my.gov.au

Click the ATO tile on your myGov home screen. Once inside the ATO portal, click Tax → Lodge return from the top menu. Select the 2024–25 income year when prompted. The system will open myTax, which is the ATO's online tax return form. This works on desktop and mobile browsers — no app download required. If you see a "Not yet available" message, the ATO usually opens lodgement from 1 July each year.

→ Open myTax

The ATO automatically pre-fills income data from your employers, banks, and government agencies — this is called "ATO pre-fill". Review every pre-filled number carefully: check that your gross income and PAYG withholding match your payment summary or payslips. If you have multiple employers, all should appear. Interest income from bank accounts and government payments (Centrelink, JobSeeker) will also be pre-filled. If anything is wrong or missing, click the field to edit it. Wait until after 31 July for the most complete pre-fill data.

Navigate to the Deductions section and enter all work-related expenses, self-education costs, charitable donations, and any other eligible deductions. Each category has its own field — use the totals from your receipts. You can claim home office expenses at 67c/hour, vehicle expenses at 88c/km (up to 5,000 km), union fees, professional subscriptions, tools, uniforms, and income protection insurance premiums. Keep all receipts for 5 years in case of an ATO audit. You don't submit receipts with your return — only keep them as records.

Once all sections are complete, myTax shows a Summary page with your estimated refund or amount owing. Check that your bank account BSB and account number are correct — this is where your refund will be deposited. Read the declaration carefully, tick the checkbox to confirm the information is true and correct, then click Submit. You'll receive a receipt number immediately. Most refunds are processed within 2 business weeks. You'll receive a Notice of Assessment by email within 28 days.

The deadline to lodge your own tax return is 31 October 2025 for the 2024–25 income year. If you miss this deadline without an extension, the ATO can issue a Failure to Lodge (FTL) penalty of $313 for every 28 days you are late, up to a maximum of $1,565. If you can't lodge in time, contact the ATO before 31 October to request a deferral — they are generally understanding for first-time requests. Using a registered tax agent automatically extends your deadline (often to May the following year). → ATO lodgement dates
